10 Tips for Better Sleep in 2026

Quality sleep is the foundation of good health, affecting everything from mood and productivity to immune function and longevity. These evidence-based tips will help you sleep better starting tonight.

Stick to a Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This consistency reinforces your body's natural sleep-wake cycle, making it easier to fall asleep and wake up refreshed.

Create a Sleep Sanctuary

Your bedroom should be cool, dark, and quiet. The ideal temperature for sleep is between 16-18°C (60-65°F). Invest in blackout curtains or a sleep mask, and consider earplugs or a white noise machine if noise is an issue.

Limit Screen Time

Blue light from phones, tablets, and computers interferes with melatonin production, the hormone that regulates sleep. Aim to stop using screens at least one hour before bed, or use blue light filtering apps.

Watch What You Consume

Avoid caffeine after noon – it can stay in your system for up to 10 hours. While alcohol might help you fall asleep, it disrupts sleep quality later in the night. Heavy meals close to bedtime can also interfere with sleep.

Exercise Regularly

Regular physical activity promotes better sleep, but timing matters. Exercise raises body temperature and releases adrenaline, which can keep you awake. Finish workouts at least 3-4 hours before bed.

Manage Stress

Practice relaxation techniques before bed, such as deep breathing, meditation, or gentle stretching. Write down worries or to-do lists to clear your mind before sleep.

Limit Naps

While short power naps can be beneficial, long or late naps can interfere with nighttime sleep. If you must nap, aim for 20-30 minutes early in the afternoon.

Invest in Bedding

Your mattress and pillows significantly impact sleep quality. Replace mattresses every 7-10 years and pillows every 1-2 years. Choose breathable, comfortable bedding materials.

Reserve Your Bed

Train your brain to associate bed with sleep. Avoid working, watching TV, or using phones in bed. This strengthens the mental connection between your bed and rest.

Track Your Sleep

Consider using a sleep tracker to identify patterns and issues. Many smartwatches and phone apps offer basic sleep tracking to help you understand your sleep habits.
